I am a Ph.D. graduate in Calculus of Variations and Geometric Measure Theory at Ecole Polytechnique (CMAP). I developed my Ph.D. thesis under the direction of Antonin Chambolle and Benoit Merlet on some variational approximations for the Branched Transport problem which is a mathematical model to study supply-demand distributions networks. Rather than using Optimal Control theory I chose the framework of Calculus of Variations and Geometric Measure Theory which allows to apply the same procedures to deal with more general problems as Plateau Type problems.

My main research interests are:

  • Analysis: Geometric Measure Theory, Calculus of Variations, Optimal and Branched Transport.
  • Variational approximations of singular energies: Ambrosio-Tortorelli approaches for energies depending on very singular objects, e.g. surfaces.
  • Numerical Optimization: of nonconvex, non linear constrained problems.
